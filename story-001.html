The Cat at Work

At the edge of the desk, the cat sat upright like a small, appointed monarch, paws folded with eerie patience before the glowing machine. The room was lit by the blue hush of screens and the pale geometry of paper trails, all of it reflected in his bright, unreadable eyes. He did not blink at the cursor. He only watched it pulse, as if it were a moth trapped in glass.

He had learned the rituals of the office quickly: the hiss of the printer, the nervous flicker of the monitor, the way humans sighed when they thought no one was listening. Tonight, he was not merely waiting; he was deciding. One tap of a paw could send a report, erase a mistake, or summon the woman who always arrived with treats and questions. His tail twitched over the desk like a metronome keeping time for a secret.
